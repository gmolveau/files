Why website monitoring is crucial for great customer experience

Website monitoring is the ongoing process of making sure your site is working as expected—and helping you know when it’s not.

There’s an important link between website monitoring and customer experience. Your site is one of your most important lines of communication with your customers, so website performance is vital to maintaining a high-quality connection.

If your website is down, filled with bugs and glitches, or delivers poor UX, customers will probably navigate away and take their business elsewhere. Poor website performance also damages your company's reputation and negatively impacts SEO rankings.

Put simply, the harder it is for customers to navigate your site, the more likely they’ll be to get frustrated and give up. That’s one of the main website monitoring advantages⁠—when customers can seamlessly and successfully interact with your site, they’re more likely to convert, which is the cornerstone of any online business.

“The biggest advantage of monitoring your website and its metrics is that you're able to spot issues quickly, and solve them fast. This can be beneficial for so many reasons, but preventing damaging impact to the bottom line of your business really stands out.”

Sean Potter
Content & SEO Strategist, Hotjar

8 excellent website monitoring tools to add to your tech stack

These eight website monitoring tools all have one thing in common—they excel at monitoring the metrics that impact customer experience the most. 

Use them strategically and you’ll have no trouble creating and maintaining a site that’s a breeze for your customers to navigate.

We’ve divided the tools into four categories:

  • Site usability

  • Site performance

  • Site health

  • Site downtime

Site usability

Site usability goes beyond just identifying whether your site works. Rather, it explores how easy it is for visitors to use and whether it serves their wants and needs.

Understanding how your customers experience your site is vital because they’re the people you want to satisfy and keep coming back for more.

Hotjar tools are perfect for collecting product experience insights to continually optimize your website

Hotjar gives you insight into exactly how users are experiencing your site. Rather than providing solely quantitative data, they give you qualitative information to round out your website monitoring and zero in on what elements your visitors enjoy as well as the issues they run into.

Hotjar tools measure metrics like session duration, page speed, and scroll depth. But where they really excel is in helping you tap into the human side of website monitoring. 

Because numbers often don’t tell the whole story, it’s a great idea to pair traditional analytics with user-centric software like Hotjar. This way, you can broaden your understanding of how your site is operating and make sure it’s seamless and intuitive for your users to navigate.

Site performance

Site performance investigates how well your site works from a technical viewpoint. It’s frequently linked to criteria like overall loading time, operational smoothness, and user interactivity.

If your site has performance issues, you’ll miss out on customers, sales, and conversions until you resolve them. That’s why diligently monitoring site performance is so essential.

An example of Pingdom in action

Pingdom is a popular software for ecommerce website analysis. In fact, as many as 88% of online consumers are less likely to return to a site after having a bad experience.

Ecommerce companies do business with customers from all over the world. It can be hard to make sure your site is performing well with so many geographic locations and time zones in the mix. 

Pingdom’s 'Real User Monitoring' tool pinpoints site performance trends across devices, regions, and time periods so you always know exactly who’s impacted by downtime or usability issues. Site health

Just like people, websites need to be in good health to operate properly. 

Site health dives into your site’s vital functions and determines how healthy they are. An unhealthy website has many errors, issues, and warnings. Some of the most harmful include:

  • Broken internal links or malformed links

  • Slow page loading times

  • Broken internal JavaScript and CSS files

  • Issues with certificates (expired, near-expired, incorrectly named)

  • Non-secure pages

These kinds of product problems put your website at a high risk of performing poorly and can make it vulnerable to hackers, so it’s vital they’re resolved quickly.

Site downtime

Website downtime happens when your site experiences some kind of outage. When users try to navigate to your site, they’re faced with an error page, are unable to complete any basic functions, or simply can’t find it at all.

Although website downtime often falls under the category of site performance, it deserves it’s own section because of how vital it is. Site downtime can seriously harm your website if it is not addressed immediately. The longer people can’t access your site, the more business you’ll lose immediately and in the long run.

If there’s one metric you should really pay attention to on your website monitoring checklist, it’s downtime.
